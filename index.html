The 7 original aspects of A.I. (1955):

  1. higher functions of the human brain
  2. Programming a computer to use general language
  3. Arranging Hypothetical neurons in a manner so that they can form concepts (machine learning)
  4. A way to determine and measure problem complexity
  5. Self-improvement (machine learning)
  6. Abstraction: defined as the quality of dealing with ideas rather than events
  7. Randomness and creativity


The official idea and definition of ai was first coined by Jay Mccartney in 1955 at the Dartmouth conference. Of course there was plenty of research work done on ai by others such as Alan Turing before this, but what they were working on was an undefined field before 1955.
Quote from John McCarthy at the Dartmouth conference: “Every aspect of learning or any other feature of intelligence can in principle be so precisely described that à machine can be made to simulate it. An attempt will be made to find how to make machines use language, form abstractions and concepts, solve kinds of problems now reserved for humans, and improve themselves.”
In essence ai is a machine with the ability to solve problems that are usually done by us humans with our natural intelligence. A computer will demonstrate a form of intelligence when it learns how to improve itself at solving these problems.
